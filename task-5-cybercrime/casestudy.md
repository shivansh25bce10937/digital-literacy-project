### CASE STUDY
# UPI/ONLINE PAYMENT FRAUD

In the fast-paced digital environment of our University, UPI has become the essential method for everything from quick canteen payments to buying used gear from seniors. This case study involves a first-year student who listed a mechanical keyboard for sale on a campus community group. Almost immediately, a potential buyer messaged them, claiming they were out of town but wanted to pay the full amount instantly to "hold" the item before anyone else could buy it.
The buyer sent a high-resolution QR code over WhatsApp and told the student that because they were using a "Corporate Business Account," the student needed to scan the code and enter their UPI PIN to "authorize the credit" into their bank account. Pressured by the buyer’s extreme sense of urgency and the excitement of a fast, profitable sale, the student scanned the code without hesitation. Even though the UPI app displayed a clear "Paying" screen with the amount listed, the student ignored the warning, under the false impression that it was just a technical glitch in the verification process.

The moment the student entered their PIN, ₹5,000 was debited from their account instead of being credited as they expected. The scammer immediately deleted the entire chat history and blocked the student’s number, leaving no trace. This incident proves that no matter how many security features a banking app has, a scammer can still succeed by manipulating a user’s lack of digital literacy and psychological state. For a Student Digital Ambassador, this highlights that the biggest vulnerability in any digital system is not the software, but the human user who hasn't been trained to recognize these common social engineering tactics.

it is important to be cautious as the chance of fraud is much much higher in UPI/ONLINE payments than person to person offline payments.
