# Social Media Do's and Don'ts Checklist
### Prepared by: Student Digital Ambassador

## Social Media Do's
* *Maintain a Professional Image:* Use a clear, professional-looking profile picture on platforms like LinkedIn to create a strong first impression for recruiters.
* *Audit Privacy Settings:* Regularly review and update your privacy settings on personal accounts to control who can access your data and posts.
* *Verify Information:* Always fact-check news, technical guides, or academic information before sharing it with your peers to ensure accuracy.
* *Network Strategically:* Politely connect with alumni and industry professionals to build a foundational professional digital footprint.
* *Model Respectful Tone:* Use a helpful and respectful tone in all public comments and threads, reflecting your role as a student representative.

## Social Media Don'ts
* *Share Private Data:* Never post sensitive personal information such as your home address, phone number, or current live location publicly.
* *Post Controversial Content:* Avoid sharing offensive or highly controversial material that could damage your future career prospects.
* *Use Unprofessional Language:* Do not use slang or offensive language when interacting with faculty, staff, or industry experts online.
* *Ignore Others' Privacy:* Do not tag classmates or colleagues in photos or videos without their explicit permission.
* *Vent Publicly:* Avoid posting frustrations about college or work in public forums, as these can be permanently visible to potential employers.
